# APAC Strategy Note — October 2024

## Strategic Context

This note summarises Acme Corporation's strategic position in the Asia-Pacific region
and outlines options for accelerating growth in FY2025.

## Current Position

APAC contributed $340M in FY2024 (14% of total company revenue), underperforming
the $390M target by $50M. Despite the shortfall, the region represents our highest
long-term growth potential, particularly in Southeast Asia.

**Key Facts:**
- APAC market share: 8.2% (FY2024), down from 9.1% (FY2023)
- Addressable Market (APAC): $4.2B (FY2024 estimate)
- Southeast Asia TAM: $85B by FY2026
- Acme Southeast Asia revenue: $41M (FY2024), representing <0.05% market share

## Southeast Asia Market Entry — Strategic Options

### Option A: Organic Expansion
- Launch dedicated Singapore HQ for SEA operations
- Hire 80–100 sales and pre-sales engineers in Q1–Q2 FY2025
- Estimated cost: $35M investment over 18 months
- Estimated revenue contribution: $65M in FY2025, $140M in FY2026
- Risk: Slow traction; competitive incumbents

### Option B: Strategic Acquisition
- Identify regional SaaS player with existing customer base
- Target profile: $20M–$50M ARR, SEA-focused, enterprise customer base
- Estimated acquisition price: $120M–$200M (4–5x ARR multiple)
- Revenue contribution: immediate; $40M+ ARR from Day 1
- Risk: Integration complexity, cultural alignment

### Option C: Partnership/Distribution
- Partner with local system integrators (e.g., regional IT distributors)
- Lower upfront cost: $8M in partner enablement and co-marketing
- Revenue share model: 30/70 (partner/Acme)
- Risk: Margin dilution, lower strategic control

## Competitive Landscape — Southeast Asia

1. **RegionalTech Inc.** — Market leader in Indonesia and Vietnam; 32% market share
2. **SingaporeSoft** — Strong in Singapore and Malaysia; enterprise focus
3. **Global Competitor A** — US-based, APAC operation out of Sydney; growing fast
4. **Acme** — Currently #4 in SEA; brand recognised but limited local presence

## Key Risks

1. **Regulatory**: Data localisation requirements in Indonesia and Vietnam require
   local data centres (est. capex $12M).

2. **Currency**: Southeast Asian currencies (IDR, VND, MYR) have high volatility;
   hedging strategy required.

3. **Talent**: Engineering and sales talent in SEA commands 15–25% premium vs global
   benchmarks due to tech sector competition.

4. **Integration Risk** (if acquisition route): Historical APAC acquisitions have
   averaged 18 months to full integration.

## Recommendation (Preliminary)

The Strategy team recommends **Option A + B hybrid**: Begin organic expansion in
Singapore immediately (Q2 FY2025) to build brand presence, while running a parallel
M&A process targeting a $20M–$30M ARR acquisition to accelerate market penetration.

Combined investment: $155M–$235M over 18 months.
Projected return: $180M+ incremental APAC revenue by FY2026.

**Board approval required** for acquisitions above $100M. Strategy team to present
full business case at Q1 FY2025 Board meeting.
